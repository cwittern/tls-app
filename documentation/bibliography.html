A Handlist of Editions of Pre-Buddhist Chinese Texts Used in the Preparation of TLS

by Christoph Harbsmeier

Manifestly, this list does not aim at completeness. I have found it useful to list up here the editions available to me in my own personal library at this point, and referred to in my work on TLS. Absences in this list do indicate that I have not had daily access to the works omitted.

The numbers on the right of each title refer to the page numbers in M. Loewe, Early Chinese Texts: A Bibliographic Guide, Berkeley: The Society for the Study of Early China, 1993, which is alphabetically arranged according to the Wade-Giles transcription of Chinese, and where the items are discussed in considerable bibliographic detail.

The decisive overall bibliographic survey is Yán Língfēng 嚴靈峰, Zhōu Qín Hàn Wèi zhūzǐ zhījiàn shūmù 周秦漢魏諸子知見書目, Revised reprint Peking: Zhōnghuáshūjú, 1993.

The best simple narrative survey of the main early works remains Burton Watson, Early Chinese Literature, New York: Columbia University Press, 1959, an immensely readable work by one of the greatest translators from Chinese.

Summaries of the contents of many of the works listed below (as well as much current bibliographic material) is conveniently assembled in Zhūzǐ bǎijiā dà cídiǎn 諸子百家大辭典, Peking: Huálíngchūbǎnshè, 1994, a work which - used judiciously - can be very helpful.
